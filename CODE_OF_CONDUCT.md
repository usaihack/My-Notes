## üîê Ethical Hacking Principles

All participants MUST adhere to these core principles:

### ‚úÖ DO

- **Practice on authorized systems only** - Use your own environments, CTF platforms, or systems with explicit permission
- **Follow responsible disclosure** - Report vulnerabilities through proper channels
- **Respect privacy and data** - Never access, modify, or disclose unauthorized information
- **Give credit** - Acknowledge sources, tools, and community contributions
- **Help others learn** - Share knowledge constructively and patiently
- **Stay current** - Keep up with legal requirements and ethical guidelines

### ‚ùå DO NOT

- **Perform unauthorized testing** - Never hack systems without explicit written permission
- **Share exploits maliciously** - Do not distribute tools or knowledge for harmful purposes
- **Engage in illegal activities** - All activities must comply with applicable laws
- **Sell or profit from vulnerabilities** - Follow responsible disclosure practices
- **Harass or threaten** - Maintain professionalism at all times

## ü§ù Community Standards

### Expected Behavior

- **Be Respectful** - Treat everyone with dignity and courtesy
- **Be Constructive** - Provide helpful feedback and meaningful contributions
- **Be Patient** - Remember everyone is learning at their own pace

## üö® Legal Compliance

### Jurisdiction Awareness

Participants must:

- Understand and comply with laws in their jurisdiction
- Recognize that computer crime laws vary by country
- Seek legal counsel when uncertain
- Accept full responsibility for their actions

### Unauthorized Access Laws

Most countries have strict laws against:

- Unauthorized computer access (e.g., US CFAA, UK Computer Misuse Act)
- Data theft and privacy violations
- Disruption of computer systems
- Creating or distributing malware

**Violating these laws can result in criminal prosecution and imprisonment.**

## üì¢ Reporting Violations

If you observe conduct that violates this Code of Conduct:

1. **Document the incident** - Take screenshots or save evidence
2. **Report through appropriate channels:**
   - For repository issues: Open a private issue or contact maintainer
   - For legal concerns: Contact local law enforcement
   - For platform violations: Report to GitHub
3. **Maintain confidentiality** - Do not publicly shame or escalate
4. **Follow up** - Check on resolution status if appropriate

### What Happens Next

- Reports are reviewed within 48 hours
- Appropriate action is taken based on severity
- Reporter is notified of outcome (when appropriate)
- Privacy of all parties is respected

## ‚öñÔ∏è Enforcement

### Consequences for Violations

Depending on severity and frequency, violations may result in:

1. **Warning** - Private written warning for minor infractions
2. **Temporary Ban** - Suspension from repository interaction (7-30 days)
3. **Permanent Ban** - Indefinite removal from community
4. **Legal Action** - Reporting to authorities for serious violations

### Appeals Process

If you believe enforcement action was unjust:

- Submit a written appeal within 14 days
- Provide evidence supporting your case
- Appeals are reviewed by an independent party
- Decisions are final after appeal review
